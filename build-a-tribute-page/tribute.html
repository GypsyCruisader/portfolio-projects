Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Here's a timeline of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1914 - Born in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1942 - Receives Ph.D. in plant pathology and genetics from the University of Minnesota
  • 1944 - Heads to Mexico to work in a project to improve wheat yields
  • 1950s-60s - Develops high-yield, disease-resistant wheat strains
  • 1968 - His efforts lead to the "Green Revolution"
  • 1970 - Wins the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1980s-90s - Continues agricultural work in Africa and Asia
  • 2009 - Dies at the age of 95
